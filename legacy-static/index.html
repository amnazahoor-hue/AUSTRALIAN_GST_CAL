Understanding GST in Australia

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a broad-based 10% tax on most goods, services, and items sold or consumed in Australia. Introduced in July 2000, it is a fundamental part of the country's taxation system.

The Formula

TO ADD GST (10%):

Total = Amount × 1.1

TO REMOVE GST (INCLUSIVE):

Base = Amount ÷ 1.1

GST Part = Amount ÷ 11

Who Needs to Register for GST?

You must register for GST if your business has a GST turnover (gross income minus GST) of $75,000 or more ($150,000 or more for non-profit organisations). Registration is also mandatory if you provide taxi or limousine travel for a fare as part of your business activity.

GST vs VAT

While Australia uses the term GST, many other countries call it Value Added Tax (VAT). Conceptually they are almost identical — a consumption tax levied at each stage of the supply chain.
